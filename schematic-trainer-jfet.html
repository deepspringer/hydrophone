Editing

Keyboard: Delete removes the selected part or wire. Esc cancels a selected terminal. Connected terminals get a halo and a short visible stub.

What students should discover

  1. Current only flows around a complete loop, from + back to –.
  2. The LED only lights when its long leg (anode) faces the + side.
  3. The resistor goes in series to hold back the current.
  4. With no resistor, too much current flows and the LED burns out.
  5. These are the same Check / Simulate / mistake-finder tools as the real labs.
Teacher note

This is a simplified educational model meant to teach the builder's interface, not a SPICE-accurate simulation. The burnout rule here is deliberately crisp ("no resistor = it dies") because that is the safe habit to teach. In reality an LED on a low source such as a 3 V coin cell may survive briefly or simply glow before failing — worth saying aloud at the bench.

Gate voltage control

Drag the slider to set VG (the voltage at the gate, measured to the – rail). For an N-channel JFET, VGS = 0 lets the most current through; pulling VGS negative pinches off the channel until the LED goes dark near VP ≈ –3 V.
