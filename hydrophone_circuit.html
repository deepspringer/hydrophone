Hydrophone Preamp Circuit Builder

Drag parts, connect terminals, run checks, and visualize the DC bias current and tiny AC signal in a piezo + 2N5457 plug-in-power microphone circuit.

1. Build the circuit

Click a part to add it. Drag parts on the board. Click one terminal, then another terminal, to place a wire.

Starter layouts

Editing

Keyboard: Delete removes the selected part or wire. Esc cancels a selected terminal. Wires are allowed to share a terminal. Connected terminals get a halo and a short visible wire stub, so hidden bends under parts do not hide the pin connection.

What students should discover

  1. Plug-in power is on the same lead as the audio signal.
  2. The JFET drain connects to the sound-card signal lead.
  3. The JFET source returns to ground through 680 Ω.
  4. The piezo and 1 MΩ resistor both go from gate to ground.
  5. The gate draws almost no DC current; it mainly senses voltage.
Teacher note

This is a simplified educational model, not a SPICE-accurate replacement. Real 2N5457 parts vary a lot, sound-card plug-in-power resistors vary, and actual pin order should be checked against the part in hand.

Source context

The reference circuit is the Dinacon 2025 piezo hydrophone buffer: a piezo disc, 2N5457 N-channel JFET, 1 MΩ gate resistor, 680 Ω source resistor, and sound-card plug-in power. Open the build notes.
