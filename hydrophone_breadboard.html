What the breadboard adds

  1. Five holes in a column are one electrical node — no wire needed between them.
  2. The center trench splits each column top-from-bottom; to carry a node across it you run a jumper.
  3. The long ± rails are your power and ground busses.
  4. A jumper only matters when it joins two different columns or a column to a rail.
Teacher note

Connectivity here is computed from the breadboard's real strip topology, not from drawn schematic links. The DC bias model and the diagnostic wording match the schematic simulator so feedback is consistent across both stages. Pin order of the 2N5457 in TO-92 is shown flat-side-facing-you as S–G–D; confirm against the datasheet for the parts in hand.
